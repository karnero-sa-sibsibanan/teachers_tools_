This developer tool was engineered to match the exact mathematical guidelines, item counts, and manual calculation structures found in official early childhood manuals, providing public educators with a streamlined workflow helper to minimize manual computation errors.

Dual-Track Compatibility: DepEd Revision vs. Official ECCD Council Manual

While the developmental frameworks are highly compatible, our tracking suite accounts for localized DepEd modifications, structural item differences, and distinct scoring shifts to ensure 100% technical accuracy. Specifically, for children aged 3.1 to 4.0 years old, the app handles the distinct scaling discrepancies found in the Expressive Language domain:

  • Raw Score of 6: Translates to a Scaled Score of 8 under the ECCD Council framework, but scales to 9 under the revised DepEd version.
  • Raw Score of 7: Translates to a Scaled Score of 10 under the ECCD Council framework, but scales to 11 under the revised DepEd version.
  • Raw Score of 8: Translates to a Scaled Score of 12 under the ECCD Council framework, but scales to 13 under the revised DepEd version.

By offering dual-track processing for both standard manual variations, this tool ensures compliant, error-free automated interpretations no matter which division mandate is active.

Structural Variances & Mathematical Coverage Mapped to the Developmental Domains:

Our calculation utility features formula fields built strictly around the domain distributions, distinct baseline item numbers, and specific skill metrics outlined across both frameworks. The application dynamically balances the original council item structure against revised DepEd checklists:

  1. Gross Motor Domain (ECCD Council: 13 Items | DepEd: 12 Items): Evaluates a child's large muscle movements, physical coordination, and balance. This includes major milestones like climbing onto furniture unassisted, running smoothly without tripping, walking backward, and navigating stairs.
  2. Fine Motor Domain (ECCD Council: 11 Items | DepEd: 10 Items): Assesses small muscle control, specifically the precision of hands and fingers. Key milestones include gripping crayons or pencils, purposeful scribbling, drawing simple shapes (like circles or a house), and unscrewing container lids.
  3. Self-Help Domain (ECCD Council: 27 Items | DepEd: 26 Items): Measures independence in daily life skills and routines. It tracks a child's progress across three essential sub-areas: feeding themselves, dressing and undressing unassisted, and developing independent toilet training skills.
  4. Receptive Language Domain (5 Items - Identical): Gauges how well a child understands spoken language and communication from others. This includes pointing to specified family members or body parts when asked and following simple one-to-two-step commands.
  5. Expressive Language Domain (8 Items - Identical): Focuses on a child's ability to vocalize and communicate thoughts, needs, and ideas. It checks for milestones like naming objects in pictures, speaking in brief grammatically correct sentences, and asking foundational queries while processing the specific age 3.1–4.0 scaling shifts.
  6. Cognitive Domain (21 Items - Identical): Tests mental development, problem-solving abilities, and logical thinking. It tracks skills like engaging in pretend play, matching or sorting objects by shape/size/color, and understanding basic analytical concepts.
  7. Social-Emotional Domain (24 Items - Identical): Examines how a child interacts with others and manages their own feelings. This involves tracking how they play with peers, share toys, show respect using standard cultural gestures (po, opo, mano), adapt to strangers, and express empathy.
